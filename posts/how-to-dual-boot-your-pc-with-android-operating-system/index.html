1. Verify and Prepare Your System for Installation#

Installing an Android operating system onto your PC is a relatively simple process. First, you must make sure your computer is capable of running an Android OS. You will need a computer with at least 4GB of RAM and a 64-bit processor. Once you have verified your PC meets the hardware requirements, you can begin the process for setting up a dual boot system with Android OS.

The next step is to create a partition on the hard drive to store the Android operating system. Using a Windows utility such as Disk Management or Partition Wizard is the easiest way to do this. You will need to create a new partition of at least 4-8GB in size.

Once the partition is created, you will need to download an Android OS ISO file. You can find these files at numerous Android download sites. Once you have downloaded the file, you will need to burn it to a CD or USB drive.

Finally, you will need to configure your boot settings to load the Android OS. To do this, restart your computer and press the boot menu key, which is usually F10, F12, or Esc. From there, you will need to select your boot device which should be the CD or USB drive that holds the Android OS ISO file. Once your computer has finished loading the Android OS, you can begin using it as the secondary operating system on your dual boot PC.
